# IBM HR Attrition Analysis

## Overview
A business intelligence project analyzing employee attrition 
across 1,470 IBM employees to identify key turnover drivers 
and deliver actionable retention recommendations for HR leadership.

**Overall attrition rate: 16.1% — vs 10–12% industry benchmark**

## Key Findings
- Overtime workers leave at **2.4× the rate** of non-overtime employees
- Sales Representatives and Lab Technicians account for **52% of departures**
- Departed employees earned **27% less** than retained peers in equivalent roles
- Attrition peaks in years **0–2** (onboarding gap) and **5–7** (promotion stagnation)
- Estimated annual cost of attrition: **$20.4 million**

## Strategic Recommendations
1. Compensation realignment — immediate pay-band review for below-market roles
2. Overtime governance — cap sustained overtime, restructure Sales/R&D resourcing
3. Enhanced onboarding — 90-day structured programme with manager check-ins
4. Career development framework — defined promotion criteria, max 12-month review cycle
5. Predictive attrition scoring — real-time risk model using satisfaction + overtime + income
